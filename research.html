RESEARCH / THREE DIRECTIONS

Integrated research
across scales.

We combine experimental observations, molecular-level analysis and genome-scale data to investigate complex biological questions.

01

GENE → PHENOTYPE

Experimental research

Establish biological function through experimental investigation.

Microbiology and molecular biology methods, including gene knockout and complementation, strain construction, inducible expression, protein purification, candidate validation and phenotypic assays, are used to characterise the functions of genes, proteins and compounds.

CloningKnockoutStrainsPurificationPhenotype
02

STRUCTURE → MECHANISM

Molecular simulation

Investigate molecular mechanisms through structure, dynamics and interactions.

Molecular dynamics simulations are used to study conformational changes, mutation effects and free-energy landscapes. Molecular docking and virtual screening support anti-infective compound discovery and the assessment of molecular binding modes.

System setupDynamicsDockingVirtual screeningFree energy

03

GENOME → INFORMATION

Bioinformatics

Analyse biological information in large-scale genomic data.

Genome assembly, gene detection, phylogenetic analysis, resistance and virulence screening, mobile genetic element analysis and data visualisation are used to investigate microbial evolution and transmission.
