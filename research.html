RESEARCH / THREE DIRECTIONS

Across scales.
Toward evidence.

Experimental observations, molecular behaviour and genome-scale data are three connected views of one research question.

01

GENE → PHENOTYPE

Experimental research

Construct, express, purify and observe.

Microbiology and molecular biology experiments use gene knockout and complementation, strain construction, inducible expression, protein purification, candidate validation and phenotype testing to establish what genes, proteins and compounds actually do.

CloningKnockoutStrainsPurificationPhenotype
02

STRUCTURE → MECHANISM

Molecular simulation

Use structure, motion and interaction to explain mechanism.

Molecular dynamics reveals conformational change, mutation effects and free-energy landscapes. Molecular docking and virtual screening extend the same structural framework to anti-infective candidate discovery and the evaluation of binding modes.

System setupDynamicsDockingVirtual screeningFree energy
03

GENOME → INFORMATION

Bioinformatics

Find biological signal in large, noisy sequence collections.

Assembly, gene detection, phylogeny, resistance and virulence screening, mobile genetic elements and visual analytics are used to reconstruct how microbial genomes evolve and spread.
